In today's global competitive market, everyday businesses are reaching new heights. Almost every day, new ideas turn into businesses. Companies frequently launch new software and products to meet changing customer needs and stay ahead of competitors.

When it comes to business, every product has information about it that serves or solves problems in today's world. For users, documentation is always the first stop to understand the product.

<!--truncate-->

## Why Documentation Matters

Customers expect instant answers. They don't want to wait for support tickets or spend time on long phone calls. Instead, they always turn to documentation first, such as:

- **Release notes** (to see what's new or improved)
- **FAQs** (for common questions)
- **Troubleshooting guides** (for solving problems)
- **How-to articles** (for step-by-step instructions)

## Documentation as the Backbone of Business

Documentation is like the backbone of any business. It makes onboarding new team members seamless, encourages knowledge sharing, and reduces the risk of losing valuable know-how when people move on. As your company grows, documentation keeps everyone aligned, so you don't lose momentum or run into costly knowledge gaps.

For larger organizations, robust documentation keeps things running smoothly across different teams and locations. It helps with compliance, streamlines training, and ensures everyone is following the same best practices. And when you launch new products or enter new markets, clear documentation helps everyone—inside and outside your company—get up to speed quickly.

## Self-Service: What Customers Love

Today's customers love self-service. They want to find answers on their own, whenever they need them, without waiting on hold or submitting a ticket. High-quality documentation—think clear instructions, troubleshooting guides, and FAQs—makes this possible.

Not only does this free up your support team, but it also builds customer loyalty. In crowded markets, being known for excellent documentation can set you apart and keep customers coming back.
