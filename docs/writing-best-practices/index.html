Writing Best Practices

This section defines the writing standards used across TechDOCS.

Use these guides to produce documentation that is:

  • clear for first-time readers,
  • reliable for production usage, and
  • maintainable across releases.

How to use this section​

Follow the modules in this order for best results:

  1. Audience and intent
  2. Content types and structure
  3. Clear, concise language
  4. Procedural writing and formatting
  5. Style consistency and review

Outcomes you should expect​

After applying this section, your docs should:

  • reduce repeated support questions,
  • improve onboarding speed,
  • standardize voice across teams, and
  • remain accurate through version updates.

Audience and Intent

Define who you're writing for and why they need your content. Create user personas and clear intent statements.

Content Types

Choose the right content format for your user need: conceptual, task, reference, tutorial, or decision content.

Content Structure

Organize information logically with proper hierarchy, navigation, and cross-references.

Clear and Concise Writing

Master clear language, eliminate jargon, and create content readers understand immediately.

Procedural Writing

Guide users through tasks step-by-step with numbered instructions and clear expected outcomes.

Commands, Code, and UI Text

Format and present code, commands, and interface elements consistently and correctly.

Style and Terminology

Build a style guide and terminology glossary to ensure consistency across all documentation.

Review and Quality Checklist

Use comprehensive checklists to review content for accuracy, clarity, consistency, and quality.
